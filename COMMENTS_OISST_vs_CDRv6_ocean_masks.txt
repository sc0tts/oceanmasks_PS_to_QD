
COMMENTS_OISST_vs_CDRv6_ocean_masks.txt

These comments refer to reprojections of data from the 25km polar stereo
  grids of NOAA CDRv5 sea ice concentration data onto the OISST quarter-
  degree latitude/longitude grid [QD].

 Reference is made to the image:

    combined_ocean_masks.gif

which is color-coded:
  White:  Both CDR and OISST indicate "ocean"
  Black:  Both CDR and OISST indicate "land"
          or OISST indicates not-ocean and the location is off-grid for CDR
   Blue:  Only the CDR mask indicates "ocean"
    Red:  Only the OISST mask indicates "ocean"


Suggestion:
  - During operational use, it is probably useful to implement an
      "average" interpolation from the CDR fields to the QD
  - Grid cells where only the CDR indicates ocean (blue) can be ignored
     - Suggest keeping track of how much ice is ignored by multiplying
       siconc by the area of these ignored grid cells?

  Northern Hemisphere:
    - There are many scattered pixels where the QD grid has ocean but there
      is no direct CDR value (red), will need to be interpolated.
      - Suggest keeping track of how much sea ice (=siconc*area) is
        added to the grid because of this operation.

    - Note:
      The Great Lakes are not ocean and the CDR does not calculate sea ice on them.
      This is different than some other sea ice concentration products, such
      as the NASA Team (eg NSIDC-0051) and Bootstrap (NSIDC-0079) products.

    - Values that are off-grid should probably be auto-set to zero
      because the CDR cannot ever provide information about these ocean
      grid cells.
      - Places where this might be an issue are:
        - in the southern part of the Sea of Okhotsk
        - south of the Northern part of Honshu (Japan)
        - Lake Ladoga (Eastern Baltic...appears as ocean in the OISST mask)
        - off the coast of the Coast Mountains in western Canada

  Southern Hemisphere:
    - It appears that the OISST land/ocean mask in the Southern Hemisphere
      does not consider the "permanent" ice shelves off Antarctica.  
      - The CDR ocean mask combines land and ice-shelf and therefore does not
        estimate sea ice over the Antarctic ice shelves, which are "ice on
        the ocean" but are not considered "sea ice"
    - Note: I used the Antarctica map from this URL for the names of the
        ice shelves mentioned below:
          https://worldmapwithcountries.net/map-of-antarctica/
    - Areas where this issue are particularly pronounced are:
      - Larsen Ice Shelf (Antarctic Peninsula)
      - Ronne Ice Shelf (Weddell Sea)
      - Ross Ice Shelf (Ross Sea)
      - Amery Ice Shelf (off of East Antarctica)
    - Suggestion:
      - It is probably best to update the OISST mask in the Southern
        Hemisphere so that it does not consider ice shelves to be
        ocean.
      - This is a bit tricky, because there are locations -- just like
        in the Northern Hemisphere -- where the QD grid does have ocean
        values for which the reprojected CDR data should be extrapolated.
